Litti chokha

Description

I still remember the first time I tried Bihari-style Litti Chokha. It was during a visit to Patna, and the flavors were a revelation. The smoky, roasted brinjal in the Chokha and the crisp, ghee-laden Litti were unlike anything I'd tasted before. It wasnâ€™t just a meal; it was an experience that transported me straight to the heart of Bihar. The Litti Chokha experience is deeply rooted in the cultural and culinary traditions of Bihar. Ingredients

    For Dough:

  • 2 cups Wheat Flour
  • 2 tsp Ghee
  • 1/2 tsp Salt
  • Water (as needed)

    • For Filling (Sattu Mixture):

  • 1 cup Sattu (roasted gram flour)
  • 1/2 cup Chopped Onion
  • 1 tbsp Chopped Ginger
  • 1 tbsp Chopped Garlic
  • 2 tbsp Chopped Green Chilli
  • 2 tbsp Chopped Coriander
  • 1/2 tsp Ajwain (carom seeds)
  • 1/2 tsp Kalonji (nigella seeds)
  • 1/2 tsp Mustard Oil
  • 1/2 tsp Amchoor Powder (dry mango powder)
  • Salt to taste
  • 1 tsp Pickle Masala

Directions

    Instructions For Chokha

  1. Prepare the Vegetables
    • Insert Garlic into Brinjal: Make small insertions on the brinjal and insert the garlic cloves into these slits.
    • Roast Vegetables: Roast the brinjal, green chillies, and tomatoes directly on an open flame until the skins are charred.
  2. Peel and Mash
    • Peel the Vegetables: Transfer the roasted brinjal, chillies, and tomatoes to a bowl of water and peel off their charred skins.
    • Mash Together: In a mixing bowl, mash the peeled vegetables together until they are well combined.
  3. Season and Mix
    • Add Seasonings: Add the chopped onion, salt, mustard oil, chopped ginger, and garlic to the mashed mixture.
    • Mix Well: Combine everything thoroughly to make a smooth and flavorful Chokha. Set aside.

    4. Instructions For Stuffing

  4. Combine Ingredients
    • Mix Sattu Mixture: In a bowl, combine sattu, chopped onion, chopped ginger, chopped garlic, chopped green chili, chopped coriander, ajwain, kalonji, mustard oil, amchur powder, salt, and pickle masala.
  5. Blend Well
    • Blend Together: Mix all the ingredients thoroughly to create a uniform stuffing. The mixture should be crumbly but moist enough to hold together when pressed.

    6. Instructions For Litti

  6. Prepare the Dough
    • Combine Ingredients: In a large bowl, add wheat flour, salt, and ghee. Rub the mixture with your fingers until it resembles breadcrumbs.
    • Knead the Dough: Gradually add water and knead into a firm dough. Let the dough rest for 10-15 minutes.
  7. Shape and Stuff Littis
    • Divide Dough: Divide the dough into small portions and roll each portion into a ball.
    • Fill and Seal: Flatten each dough ball, place a spoonful of the sattu stuffing in the center, and gather the edges to seal the dough around the filling. Shape into smooth balls.
  8. Cook the Littis
    • Prepare the Cooker: Heat a pressure cooker (remove the whistle for safety) and grease the inside with oil.
    • Cook the Littis: Place the stuffed littis in the cooker and cook on medium-low flame for 25-30 minutes, turning occasionally to ensure even cooking. The littis should be golden brown and crispy.

    9. Instructions For Green Chutney

  9. Prepare Ingredients
    • Combine in Blender: Add coriander leaves, mint leaves, tomato, green chilies, ginger, garlic cloves, salt, and lemon juice to a blender.
  10. Blend Until Smooth
    • Blend: Blend the ingredients until you get a smooth and consistent chutney. Adjust seasoning to taste.

    11. Instructions For Chokha

  11. Prepare the Vegetables
    • Insert Garlic into Brinjal: Make small insertions on the brinjal and insert the garlic cloves into these slits.
    • Roast Vegetables: Roast the brinjal, green chilies, and tomatoes directly on an open flame until the skins are charred.
  12. Peel and Mash
    • Peel the Vegetables: Transfer the roasted brinjal, chilies, and tomatoes to a bowl of water and peel off their charred skins.
    • Mash Together: In a mixing bowl, mash the peeled vegetables together until they are well combined.
  13. Season and Mix
    • Add Seasonings: Add the chopped onion, salt, mustard oil, chopped ginger, and garlic to the mashed mixture.
    • Mix Well: Combine everything thoroughly to make a smooth and flavorful Chokha. Set aside.

    14. Instructions For Serving

  14. Serve the Littis
    • Serve the crispy and golden-brown Littis hot, along with the smoky Chokha and fresh Green Chutney. Enjoy the authentic flavors of Bihari cuisine!
